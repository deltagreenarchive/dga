### Pudel
- A single Agent notices framed, black-and-white photographs on a wall. 
- They portray an odd array of people staring into the camera, their faces blank. Each of these people is dressed in 1930s garb and holds odd-shaped bottles of various designs. 
- A single name rides the lip of each photograph, written in ink with a steady hand: 
	- A. DARIBONDI, 
	- E. LOSETTE, 
	- J. LINZ, 
	- E. MOSEBY, 
	- D. CARVER, 
	- G. TOPCHICK, 
	- H. LUN DINE, 
	- and others.
- The last photograph shows a single dark bottle sitting on the floor, and the name on the bottom is that of the Agent with the highest [[Corruption]] rating. This is the Agent’s bottle from the Whisper Labyrinth. 
- If the photograph is touched, the Agent gains +1 [[Corruption]] and hears in a near-silent voice whisper,

### The Dance 
- The Agents find an entire ballroom full of human-size marionettes. 
- Marionette couples sway and dance, their strings disappearing up into the dark. Marionette waiters bring empty glasses to tables of revelers. 
- A marionette band seems to play a soothing melody (actually a Victrola under the stage, playing an unmarked record). 
	- This costs 0/1D4 SAN from the unnatural. 
- The marionettes completely ignore the Agents. 
- Inspecting the darkened ceiling, perhaps by stacking tables, reveals that the strings are locked on tracks without any operator, moving on their own. 
	- This discovery costs 1/1D6 SAN from the unnatural.


Doktor
- The Agents see a tall, bulky, balding Caucasian man in a short-sleeved, button-down shirt and a Boston Red Sox windbreaker. 
- He emerges from a door in front of the Agents and walks quickly away from them. 
- He does not stop or turn around, and does not acknowledge them. He enters another door ahead, clearly enunciating “twenty-seven” as he does so. 
- If this door shuts with no Agents on the other side and they open it, it reveals an empty hotel room with no exits. 
	- This costs 0/1 SAN from the unnatural. 
- If the Agents wait, the man emerges after a minute or two. 
- If they keep pace with the man, they find him going through doors and upping the count by one as he enters and closes each door behind him. 
- He does not respond to questioning but only nods and smiles. 
- If Agents attempt to subdue the man, he fights and struggles, using lethal force if necessary. Then he does his best to keep going door to door. (See DR. RICHARD F. DALLAN, FACILITY ADMINISTRATOR, BY DAY on page 178.)
